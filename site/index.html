The problem

A local model can speak.
That doesn’t mean it can know.

01 Model alone

“Looks good—go for it!”

  • May miss the relevant data
  • May invent a detail
  • Cannot show its work
02 Model + Operon

“Go early. Rain probability rises after 1 PM. [S1]”

  • Retrieves authorized local facts
  • Checks output against your contract
  • Returns sources and an execution trace

The runtime

More than a prompt.
A disciplined loop.

01

Understand

Simple requests move fast. Complex ones become a typed plan with clear answer requirements.

02

Ground

The host selects the right local context: files, a weather snapshot, app state, or user-approved memory.

03

Generate

Your inference engine produces structured output. Operon never owns your model or your data.

04

Verify & repair

Check citations, confidence, and your typed business rules. Repair once—or return a safe failure.

Built for real apps

Small models. Serious boundaries.

Operon makes a model useful inside a product without turning the model into the product’s source of truth.

⌘

App-owned authority

Your app performs retrieval, calculations, permissions, and side effects. The model never gets ambient access.

◌

Local by default

Wrap Ollama, WebLLM, Apple Foundation Models, llama.cpp, or an explicitly approved cloud provider.

↯

Bounded repair

When an answer violates the output contract, Operon supplies the exact failure and gives the model one focused retry.

◎

Inspectable traces

Every result carries the plan, sources, confidence, repair state, and trace needed to understand what happened.

local terminal
from operon import Operon, LocalDocuments

runtime = Operon.wrap(
  model=your_local_model,
  grounding=LocalDocuments("./app-facts"),
)

result = runtime.run("Answer using local facts")
print(result.answer, result.sources)
